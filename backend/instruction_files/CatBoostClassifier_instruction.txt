MODEL_SELECTION_REASONING:
CatBoostClassifier should be prioritised for tabular classification tasks where feature subsets are predominantly numerical, class distributions are balanced, and at least 2–3 features with discriminative power are available. It handles categorical features well when encoding is enabled, and scales reliably to high-dimensional datasets. It is particularly well suited when the dataset has 30+ total features, enabling the selection of high-signal subsets.

MODEL_AVOIDANCE_REASONING:
Avoid CatBoostClassifier for single-feature classification tasks, heavily imbalanced datasets without resampling, or feature subsets dominated by categorical features without proper encoding. It should not be the first choice when the dataset falls in the 20–24 total feature range without clear numerical signal, or when inference speed and model simplicity are higher priorities than accuracy.
