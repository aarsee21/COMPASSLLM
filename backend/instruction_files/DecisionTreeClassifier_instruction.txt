MODEL_SELECTION_REASONING:
DecisionTreeClassifier should be prioritized for structured classification tasks where interpretability is important and feature sets contain strong discriminative signals. It performs well across both numerical and categorical datasets, particularly with 3-feature subsets and balanced class distributions. It achieves perfect accuracy on well-separated datasets (heart disease, iris, wine) and is robust to feature scaling differences, making it easy to apply with minimal preprocessing overhead.

MODEL_AVOIDANCE_REASONING:
Avoid DecisionTreeClassifier for single-feature subsets or datasets with weak, noisy, or correlated low-information features (e.g., elemental composition fields like Fe, Si, Ca). Without depth constraints or pruning, it is prone to overfitting on small or noisy samples. It also underperforms on imbalanced datasets compared to ensemble alternatives, and its higher variance (std: 0.11) relative to boosted models makes it less reliable in uncertain feature selection scenarios.
