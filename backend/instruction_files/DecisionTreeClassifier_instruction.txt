MODEL_SELECTION_REASONING:
DecisionTreeClassifier should be prioritised when model interpretability is required alongside reasonable accuracy, particularly on balanced datasets with 2–3 numerical features carrying strong discriminative signal. It is preprocessing-light (insensitive to scaling and encoding) and performs well when feature subsets are drawn from numerically rich datasets. Best suited for quick baseline establishment and explainable classification pipelines.

MODEL_AVOIDANCE_REASONING:
Avoid DecisionTreeClassifier for single-feature classification tasks, heavily imbalanced datasets, or feature subsets dominated by categorical features. Without depth constraints or pruning, it overfits on low-signal or noisy feature combinations. When accuracy is the primary objective over interpretability, CatBoostClassifier or ensemble methods should be preferred. Also avoid when the dataset falls in the difficult 20–24 total feature range without clear high-signal numerical features.
