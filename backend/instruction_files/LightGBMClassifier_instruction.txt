HYPERPARAMETER_PATTERNS:
•  no regularisation (reg_alpha=0.0, reg_lambda=0.0) means the model relies purely on
  ensemble depth and learning rate for generalisation
•  colsample_bytree=1.0 and subsample=1.0 disable stochastic boosting — all runs use
  deterministic full-data gradient updates, reducing variance but limiting diversity between trees
•  num_leaves=31 provides moderate tree complexity — sufficient for most 2–3 feature subsets
  but may underfit on very high-signal low-dimensional tasks where deeper splits are beneficial
•  class_weight=None means imbalanced class distributions are not internally corrected,
  explaining the 6.9% accuracy drop on imbalanced datasets
•  performance variation is entirely data-driven — no tuning was applied across any run

MODEL_SELECTION_REASONING:
LightGBMClassifier should be prioritised when probability calibration quality (ROC AUC) matters
as much as accuracy, or when datasets contain a mix of numerical and categorical features where
full encoding cannot be guaranteed. It is the most stable model at 3-feature subsets (lowest std)
and the least sensitive to encoding, making it robust across varied preprocessing pipelines.
Particularly well suited for balanced datasets with 30+ total features or compact 5–9 feature
datasets where signal density is high. Its gradient boosting ensemble provides reliable
residual correction even on moderately noisy feature combinations.

MODEL_AVOIDANCE_REASONING:
Avoid LightGBMClassifier for single-feature classification tasks, particularly on imbalanced data
where the size=1 imbalanced combination produces mean accuracy of 0.569 with the highest observed
instability (std=0.234). Without class_weight correction, it does not internally compensate for
class imbalance. For the 20–24 total feature range, all models including LightGBM struggle
(mean 0.740), and ensemble alternatives with regularisation or resampling should be considered.
When interpretability is the primary requirement over accuracy or probability ranking,
DecisionTreeClassifier remains a simpler and more transparent alternative.
